## Governing Principle

Claims are tiered by strength of evidence:

| Tier | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Tier A** | Arithmetically proven or directly demonstrated from primary folio evidence |
| **Tier B** | Strongly supported by folio evidence; convergent lines of argument |
| **Tier C** | Speculative proposal; hypothesis under investigation |
| **Tier X** | Withdrawn, falsified, or superseded |

## Priority Claims Register

### PC-01: Flintlock Prototype
**Tier B**  
Madrid Codex I contains design evidence for a flintlock ignition mechanism predating the traditionally credited inventor Marin le Bourgeoys by approximately 115 years.  
*Folio basis: documented in da-vinci-mechanica white paper.*  
*Falsification condition: demonstration that the mechanism serves a different function, or that a prior independent design predates the Codex folio.*

---

### PC-02: Resonant Impulse Accumulation Theory
**Tier B**  
The Madrid Codex I undulated wheel system constitutes a documented resonant impulse accumulation mechanism — a universal pulse/regulation principle operating across drive, transmission, and output subsystems.  
*Folio basis: multiple folios; cross-referenced in Master Index.*  
*Falsification condition: demonstration that undulated wheels function as simple escapements only.*

---

### PC-03: Combined Clock-and-Orrery Modular Platform
**Tier B**  
The Madrid Codex I machine architecture includes an integrated clock-and-orrery modular platform — the first documented design combining timekeeping and planetary modelling in a single mechanical system.  
*Folio basis: documented in da-vinci-mechanica Chapters VI–VIII.*

---

### PC-04: First Multi-Output Parallel Analogue Computing Platform
**Tier B**  
The Madrid Codex I central shaft system constitutes the first documented multi-output parallel analogue computing platform, in which multiple independent output channels are driven simultaneously from a single regulated power source.  
*Folio basis: central shaft doctrine established across folio sequence.*  
*Note: "Computing platform" is used in the strict mechanical sense of a system that performs simultaneous parallel calculations through gear ratios and regulated transmission.*

---

### PC-05: Lager (6) Nine-Stage Friction Cascade
**Tier A**  
The identified nine-stage friction cascade produces a theoretical mechanical advantage reduction ratio of 24⁹ (approximately 2.8 × 10¹²). This is an arithmetically verified calculation from the documented stage configuration.  
*Falsification condition: demonstration that fewer than nine stages are present, or that stages are not arranged in cascade.*

---

### PC-06: Chiaravalle Abbey Geographic Anchor
**Tier A**  
The inscription "Poli in poli, chome in Chiaravalle" constitutes the most important geographic and dating anchor in the forty-folio sequence analysed. It confirms the Codex was composed with reference to Chiaravalle abbey and provides a terminus post quem for the relevant folio sequence.  
*Status: confirmed primary source text.*

---

### PC-07: Arabic Spatial Orientation (Recto-Verso Arabic Doctrine)
**Tier B**  
The Madrid Codex I is written in Arabic spatial orientation. Individual diagrams cannot be used to reconstruct functioning machines in isolation; they must be read in sequence according to the Arabic spatial logic of the codex as a whole.  
*Methodological implication: all prior reconstruction attempts based on individual folio diagrams are incomplete.*
