## 500-Word Abstract

**Leonardo da Vinci and the Architecture of Transfer: A Systems-Engineering Reassessment**

This project presents a systematic reassessment of Leonardo da Vinci's Madrid Codex I (BNE Ms. 8937, c.1493–1497) as a unified mechanical treatise, supplemented by the Madrid Codex II (BNE Ms. 8936, c.1503–1505) as a universal architectural framework. The analysis draws directly on primary BNE Interactivo transcripts, cross-referenced against the RWTH Aachen critical edition, producing the first full English-language analytical engagement with all 192 folios of Madrid I.

The central finding is that Madrid Codex I is not a collection of isolated machines but a systematic mechanics encyclopedia organised around a central shaft architecture and a modular platform model. Six original priority claims are formally established: a complete flintlock ignition sequence (BNE 5907, approximately 115 years before the credited invention of the flintlock by Marin le Bourgeoys); the first identification of synchronised resonant impulse accumulation as both the theoretical foundation of escapement physics and the governing counter-argument to perpetual motion; the identification of Madrid I as a combined clock-and-orrery modular platform with astronomically coherent gear ratios including a 29.5-day lunar synodic period; the systematic development of the Chiaravalle Cistercian abbey clock as a precision dating anchor and real-world mechanical precedent; the identification of ink seepage between folios as intentional mechanical design registration; and the first systematic identification of the over-centre toggle-lock principle in engineering literature.

A companion study, *Principia Omnium Universale*, argues that Leonardo da Vinci's surviving corpus is governed by a universal architecture of motion, medium, form, and memory, with Madrid Codex II as the primary proof-case. The root law — *La cosa che ssi move acquissta tanto di spatio quanto ella ne lasscia* — recurs in confirmed textual form across six instances in five stages of the codex, and is structurally consistent with what later became the conservation of energy.

A secondary and experimental thesis, *Figura Ultima*, proposes that the Turin Shroud image is consistent with a machine-mediated photothermal transfer process of a type Leonardo demonstrably understood and documented. This thesis is presented as a testable model requiring adversarial review, not a proven claim. Its central quantitative argument — the 7.1 scalar ratio between the Vitruvian Man and the Shroud's dimensional geometry — is a mathematical calculation that either holds or does not hold at the stated precision.

An optical study, *SolarScorch*, investigates whether Codex Atlanticus f.673r documents a solar geometry relevant to the Palazzo Vecchio Lion Rooms. A confirmed near-noon street-axis alignment is documented; a required shadow bearing of 156.4° remains an unresolved anomaly with three candidate explanations.

All findings are presented as testable models, candidate frameworks, and interpretive hypotheses for specialist critique. No institutional endorsement is implied or claimed.
