## Governing Principle

Every major claim in this corpus must have a stated falsification pathway. A claim that cannot be falsified is not a scholarly claim. These protocols define the conditions under which each major claim would be conclusively disproved.

## Priority Claims — Falsification Conditions

### PC-1: Flintlock Prototype (BNE 5907)

**Claim:** Madrid Codex I contains a complete flintlock ignition sequence, approximately 115 years before Marin le Bourgeoys.

**Falsification conditions:**
1. A dated primary source is found describing an equivalent complete flintlock mechanism before c.1493 — the earliest possible Madrid I date.
2. The BNE 5907 transcription is shown to contain a translation error that removes one or more of the six named components.
3. The codex dating (c.1493–1497) is revised to post-1610 through new codicological evidence.

**Current status:** Not falsified. Falsification condition 1 has not been met in any published scholarship reviewed.

### PC-3: Combined Clock-and-Orrery Platform

**Claim:** Madrid Codex I documents, for the first time, a combined clock-and-orrery platform with astronomically coherent gear ratios including a 29.5-day lunar synodic period.

**Falsification conditions:**
1. A prior source is found that treats the Madrid I clock and orrery components as a unified platform.
2. The gear cascade 12:24:4:4:59 is shown not to yield a 1:29.5 ratio through arithmetical error.
3. The BNE folios cited (5902, 5906, 5890) are shown not to contain the claimed gear sequences.

**Note:** This claim has the highest TYPE_C loading and would most benefit from independent physical examination.

## Falsification Infrastructure

All claims are subject to the evidence tier system in `ECS_EVIDENCE_CLASSIFICATION_REGISTER.md`.

TYPE_D claims (speculative) are assumed falsifiable by definition and require explicit falsification pathways before any publication.

TYPE_C claims (interpretive) require at minimum two independent Type-A sources that contradict the interpretation.

TYPE_A claims (primary observed) are falsified by: (a) transcription error, (b) misidentification of folio, (c) dating revision of manuscript.
