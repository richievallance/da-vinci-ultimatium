## A

**Arabic Spatial Orientation** — The reading direction of Madrid Codex I. The codex is written right-to-left in the Arabic spatial convention, meaning the machine design sequence must be read accordingly. Individual diagrams cannot be used in isolation. See: *Recto-Verso Arabic Doctrine*.

## C

**Central Shaft Doctrine** — The governing structural principle of Madrid Codex I: all mechanical subsystems (Drive, Transmission, Regulation, Oscillation, Output, Structural) feed, drive, or regulate a master central power spine. No subsystem is independent.

**Chiaravalle Anchor** — The inscription *"Poli in poli, chome in Chiaravalle"* within Madrid Codex I. The most important geographic and dating anchor in the analysed folio sequence. Establishes the Codex was composed with reference to Chiaravalle abbey. Classification: Tier A.

## D

**Device Classes (Six)** — The six functional categories of mechanisms in Madrid Codex I:
1. Drive — primary power input
2. Transmission — power routing
3. Regulation — speed/force control
4. Oscillation — reciprocating elements
5. Output — external work delivery
6. Structural — load-bearing framework
