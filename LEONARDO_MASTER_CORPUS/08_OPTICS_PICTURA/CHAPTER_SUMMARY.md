## Historical Context

Leonardo's optical investigations intensified during his Rome period (1513–1516) and continued in France. The SolarScorch study focuses on Codex Atlanticus f.673r (CA673) — a solar geometry study linked to the Lion Rooms of the Palazzo Vecchio, Florence, and the feast of Saint John the Baptist (24 June 1518, Julian calendar).

## What This Chapter Covers

- Solar bearing tests for the Saint John optical alignment
- Atmospheric refraction modelling
- KML geometric overlays of the Palazzo Vecchio
- The **156.4° unresolved anomaly** — documented, not explained

## Open Issue

The 156.4° bearing remains anomalous. It is flagged but not used to support any claim until resolved.
