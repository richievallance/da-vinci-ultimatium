## Historical Context

Leonardo composed Madrid Codex I (BNE MS 8937) during his first period of service to Ludovico Sforza in Milan, approximately 1482–1499. This was his most sustained period of pure mechanical investigation. The Codex is treated by the Da Valenca Archive not as a working notebook but as a publication-ready master engineering treatise with a confirmed structure of at least nine books.

## What This Chapter Covers

The Da Valenca reconstruction of Madrid Codex I establishes:

- The **central shaft doctrine** — all subsystems feed a master power spine
- **Six device classes** — Drive, Transmission, Regulation, Oscillation, Output, Structural
- The **seepage alignment methodology** — ink bleed-through between physically adjacent leaves as intentional design registration
- The **Recto-Verso Arabic Doctrine** — the Codex reads in Arabic spatial orientation; individual diagrams cannot be used in isolation
- The **Chiaravalle anchor** — geographic and dating confirmation (Tier A)
