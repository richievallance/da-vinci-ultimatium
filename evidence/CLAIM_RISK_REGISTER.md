## Risk Classification

| Risk Level | Definition |
|---|---|
| LOW | Claim is grounded in verified primary source; method is reproducible; prior scholarship supports or does not contradict |
| MODERATE | Claim is well-grounded but interpretive extension goes beyond direct evidence; requires careful presentation |
| HIGH | Claim relies on convergent inference without direct evidence; could be contested; requires extensive qualification |
| CRITICAL | Claim could not be defended if primary evidence is reinterpreted; must be marked speculative in all public-facing text |

## Language Discipline — Banned and Preferred Phrases

### Banned (never use without explicit TYPE qualification)

- "proves"
- "definitively created"
- "definitively shows"
- "hidden code"
- "cipher"
- "Leonardo definitely made the Shroud"
- "institutionally validated"
- "confirmed beyond doubt"
- "conspiracy"

### Required for TYPE_C interpretive claims

- "candidate framework"
- "testable model"
- "interpretive hypothesis"
- "most probable explanation consistent with"
- "provisional reconstruction"
- "systematic identification"

### Required for TYPE_D speculative claims

- "working hypothesis"
- "speculative model"
- "requires further investigation"
- "cannot be confirmed from available evidence"
- "candidate mechanism"
