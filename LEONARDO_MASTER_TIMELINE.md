## c.1452 — Birth
- **15 April 1452**: Born, Anchiano, near Vinci, Republic of Florence
- Illegitimate son of Ser Piero da Vinci (notary) and Caterina
- 2023: Carlo Vecce's discovery regarding Caterina's background (integrated into figura-ultima)
- 2026 (Jan): Da Vinci DNA Project haplogroup findings (integrated into figura-ultima)

## c.1466–1476 — Verrocchio Workshop, Florence
- Apprenticed to Andrea del Verrocchio, Florence
- Guild enrolment: 1472
- Collaborates on Verrocchio works including Baptism of Christ (angel attributed to Leonardo)

## 1476–1478 — Gap Years [PARTIALLY UNCERTAIN]
- Movements not fully documented
- Ottoman letters (Richter §§1336–7): possible presence in Armenia/Syria region [HYPOTHESIS — see figura-ultima]
- Relevance: proposed window for Turin Shroud production c.1476–1478 [HYPOTHESIS — Tier C]

## c.1478–1482 — Independent Florence
- Adoration of the Magi begun (unfinished, Uffizi)
- St Jerome in the Wilderness (Pinacoteca Vaticana)

## c.1482 — Departure to Milan
- Moves to Milan; enters service of Ludovico Sforza
- Letter to Sforza listing engineering capabilities

## c.1482–1499 — First Milanese Period
- **Madrid Codex I composed** (BNE MS 8937) — master engineering treatise
  → [da-vinci-mechanica](https://github.com/richievallance/da-vinci-mechanica)
- Key Codex anchor: *"Poli in poli, chome in Chiaravalle"* — geographic and dating anchor (Tier A)
- Anatomical studies begin c.1489
- **c.1490**: Vitruvian Man (Gallerie dell'Accademia, Venice)
- **c.1494–1498**: Last Supper, Santa Maria delle Grazie

## 1499 — French Invasion
- Ludovico Sforza falls; Leonardo leaves Milan
- Takes notebooks; departures to Mantua, Venice, then Florence

## 1499–1506 — Wandering and Florence
- **c.1500**: Venice (studies architecture and fortification)
- **c.1503–1506**: Florence — Mona Lisa begun; Battle of Anghiari (lost)

## 1506–1513 — Second Milanese Period
- Returns to Milan under French
- **Madrid Codex II composed** (BNE MS 8936) — universal architecture thesis
  → [principia-mechanica](https://github.com/richievallance/principia-mechanica) / [corpus-leonardianum](https://github.com/richievallance/corpus-leonardianum)
- Major anatomical collaboration with Marcantonio della Torre c.1510

## 1513–1516 — Rome
- Under Giuliano de' Medici, Belvedere
- Optical experiments; mirror manufacture; Codex Atlanticus material
- CA673 (Codex Atlanticus f.673r): solar geometry, CA673/Saint John study
  → [solarscorch](https://github.com/richievallance/solarscorch)

## 1516–1519 — France (Amboise)
- Invited by Francis I; given Château du Clos Lucé
- **24 June 1518**: Feast of Saint John — CA673 solar alignment [HYPOTHESIS — see solarscorch]
- **1517**: Cardinal Luigi d'Aragona visit; three paintings documented
- **2 May 1519**: Death at Amboise
