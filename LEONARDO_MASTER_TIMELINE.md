## MECHANICAL DEVELOPMENT PHASES

### Phase 1 — Pre-systematic (c.1480–1487)
Scattered mechanism drawings; no unified system. Pre-Madrid.

### Phase 2 — Systematic Composition (c.1487–1497)
Madrid Codex I active. Unified mechanical treatise. Central shaft architecture. Gear taxonomy. Escapements. Regulation systems.

### Phase 3 — Geometric-Hydraulic Integration (c.1494–1506)
Madrid Codex II. Geometry meets hydraulics. World-scale and body-scale unified. Arno/Horse theorem.

### Phase 4 — Anatomical Convergence (c.1506–1513)
Body as machine. Machine as body. Anatomical folios cross-reference mechanical systems.

### Phase 5 — Optical Synthesis (c.1513–1519)
Optics, projection, solar geometry. Mirror systems. Late-period unification of light and mechanics.
